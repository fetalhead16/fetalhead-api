   At first, researchers tackled fetal head segmentation with
traditional image analysis tools. For instance, Hadlock et al. [5]
introduced a method that automatically fits elliptical shapes to
the data. Following this, models evolved - some adopted active
contours, others relied on gradient-based snakes or used Hough
transforms to spot ellipses. While these worked well when scan
quality was high, they often failed when faced with common
issues like sound wave shadows, changes in how the scanner
was angled, or grainy patterns naturally found in ultrasound
scans.
   Ellipse fitting via Ramanujan’s formula guides classic HC
estimation in biometric systems [11]. Though regression mod-
els reach under 2 mm error, as shown by Peng et al. [12],
